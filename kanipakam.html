Kanipakam Temple, An Ancient Lord Ganesha Temple in Chittoor

May 12, 2023, 7:05 PM IST
Blog By: Rishika Singh

Did you know about the Ganpati temple in Kanipakam village, located in the Chittoor district of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh?

Kanipakam Ganesh Temple was established in the early 11th century CE by the Chola King Kulothunga Chola I and was later enhanced even more in 1336 by the Emperors of the Vijayanagara dynasty. Kanipakam Temple is one of the holy abodes of Lord Vinayaka (Ganesha) and is also known as Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple. It is present on the banks of river Baahuda, in Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh. Ganesha is the controller of Ganas, the remover of obstacles, and the Lord of wisdom. It is usually visited when one is visiting Tirumala Balaji Temple.

What is the Significance Kanipakam Temple?

The importance of the Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple is that Lord Ganesh is known as "Varasiddhi Vinayaka" there because he grants his worshippers' heartfelt requests when they make sincere and devoted prayer offerings. In numerous cases, a person has also willingly confessed to wrongdoing upon entering the temple.

Many people regard the hallowed shrine of the Kanipakam temple to be a defender of justice and truth. When someone bathes in holy water, they are enveloped in the atmosphere of the Divine Almighty.

Even today, disputes between neighbours are settled at the temple by the two parties signing an oath known as a "Pramanam," which requires them to testify before the Lord while taking a bath in the Pushkarini.

How to Reach Kanipakam Temple?

Via Air: You can avail direct flights to Tirupati from Hyderabad and Chennai only.

Via Rail: You can avail a direct train from Tirupati to Chittoor.

Via Road: Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (APSRTC) runs daily buses between Bangalore and Chittoor. Direct buses are available to Kanipakam Vinayaka Temple, 70 km away from Tirupati.

Where to Stay near Kanipakam Temple?

You do not have to worry about the Kanipakam temple accommodation as the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams Trust manages the operations of 14 rooms for devotees, while the Temple Management manages 6 guest rooms. Another 100-room hotel complex is now being built. Along with the accommodations offered by the temple, Chittoor city offers visitors a number of high-quality private hotels and lodges.
